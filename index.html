Helpful Notes

  • Peanuts: Only unsalted, aflatoxin-tested bird peanuts. Offer crushed/through a mesh feeder and avoid whole peanuts during breeding season to prevent choking risks for chicks.
  • Dried larvae (mealworms or BSFL): Rehydrate in warm water for 10â€“20 minutes to improve uptake and hydration. Always provide fresh water nearby.
  • Suet/fat: Skip plastic net bags (leg/foot entanglement). Use feeders or crumble on a tray. Plain fat works in cold weather; insect blends attract insectivores.
  • Raisins/currants: Toxic to dogs. Place where pets cannot access and clear leftovers at dusk.
  • Nyjer: Needs a fine-port tube feeder to avoid spillage. Likely visitors in Mannheim include Goldfinch; in some winters, Siskin. Greenfinch may use it occasionally.
  • Hygiene: Clean feeders/trays every 2â€“3 days in warm weather (weekly in winter) with 10% bleach, rinse, and dry. If you see sick birds (fluffed, drooling, difficulty swallowing), remove food and clean thoroughly for 2 weeks.
  • Placement: Put feeders near cover (about 2â€“3 m) but with clear escape routes. Use window decals or place feeders within 1 m of windows to reduce collisions.
